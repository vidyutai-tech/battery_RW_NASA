Two-Stage Fine-Tuning for Cross-Cell Transfer

RW9 source twin → RW10 / RW11 / RW12  |  Limited target data (20–60%)

Problem: Standard loss is voltage-dominated (100·MSEV + 1·MSET). With a tiny fine-tune LR (5×10−7), the temperature head barely adapts when transferring to a new cell.

Each stage uses early stopping on validation loss. Checkpoints saved at 20%, 40%, and 60% of target-cell training data.

Stage 1

Output-Head Warmup (backbone frozen)

  • Freeze transformer + embeddings → train only 3 output layers (~3K params)
  • Loss: 1·MSEV + 100·MSET + 5·(1 − PearsonT)
  • LR ≈ 5×10−4 (1000× stage-2 LR) — safe with frozen backbone
  • Up to 150 epochs — re-calibrate temperature scale without breaking voltage physics
Stage 2

Full Fine-Tuning (all layers unfrozen)

  • Unfreeze all ~2M parameters
  • Balanced loss: 50·MSEV + 50·MSET + 5·PearsonT
  • LR = 5×10−7 — gentle joint refinement on limited target data
  • Up to 500 epochs — optimize voltage & temperature together
